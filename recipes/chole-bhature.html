Chole-bhature

Description

Chole Bhature is a classic North Indian dish that pairs spicy chickpea curry (chole) with soft, fluffy deep-fried bread (bhature). It's a popular street food and festive meal, especially in Punjab and Delhi. The Chole is rich with aromatic spices, ginger, and green chilies, while the bhature is made from refined flour and yogurt, puffed to perfection. Served with sliced onions, lemon wedges, and sometimes pickles, this dish is a hearty and flavorful indulgence.

Ingredients

  • For chole (chickpea curry):

    1. Chickpeas
    2. Spices
    3. Tomato & onion
    4. Oil,water,salt

  • For bhature (fried bread):

    1. All-purpose flour (maida)
    2. Semolina (sooji)
    3. Baking powder
    4. Oil,water,salt

  • Optional Ingredients

    1. Sliced onions
    2. Pickles

Steps

  1. Soak chickpeas overnight and pressure cook until soft.
  2. Saut√© cumin seeds, bay leaf, chopped onions, ginger, garlic, and green chili in oil.
  3. Add tomatoes and cook until soft, then mix in turmeric, chili powder, coriander powder, and salt.
  4. Add cooked chickpeas and simmer with water until the curry thickens.
  5. Finish with garam masala and garnish with fresh coriander.
  6. For bhature, mix flour, semolina, yogurt, baking powder, salt, and water into a soft dough.
  7. Let the dough rest for a few hours, then roll into discs.
  8. Deep fry until golden and puffed.
