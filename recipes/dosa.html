Masala Dosa

Description

Masala dosa is a beloved South Indian dish known for its crispy texture and flavorful filling. It consists of a thin, golden-brown crepe made from fermented rice and urad dal (black gram) batter, rolled around a spiced potato filling. Traditionally served on a banana leaf, it's accompanied by tangy chutneys and aromatic sambar. The dish is not only a staple in South Indian cuisine but also a favorite across India and beyond, celebrated for its balance of textures, spices, and wholesome ingredients.

Ingredients

  • For the dosa batter

    1. Rice
    2. Urad Dal
    3. Fenugreek Seeds

  • For the masala

    1. Potatoes
    2. Onion
    3. Spice: Green chili,turmeric powder,coriander leaves
    4. Mustard seeds

  • Optional Ingredients

    1. Sambar
    2. Chutney

Steps

  1. Soak rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds in water for several hours.
  2. Grind the soaked mixture into a smooth batter and allow it to ferment overnight.
  3. Prepare the masala by saut√©ing onions, green chili, mustard seeds, curry leaves, and turmeric powder in oil.
  4. Add boiled and mashed potatoes to the mixture, season with salt, and cook until well combined.
  5. Heat a flat pan or tawa, pour a ladle of batter, and spread it thin into a circular shape.
  6. Drizzle a little oil around the edges and cook until golden and crisp.
  7. Place a portion of the potato masala in the center of the dosa.
  8. Fold the dosa and serve hot with coconut chutney, tomato chutney, or sambar.
